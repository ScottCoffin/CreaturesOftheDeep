The West Sacramento Experimental Trophic Laboratory (W.E.T. LAB) cordially invites selected participants to attend an active research investigation at Station 744, a field site dedicated to the study of marine and marine-adjacent organisms' adaptation to complex environmental stimuli, interspecies interaction, and emergent evolutionary behavior under dynamic social conditions.

The W.E.T. LAB has been thrilled to report that recent experimental outcomes have exceeded our anonymous funder's expectations. Several organisms have demonstrated unexpected intelligence, cooperation, and (most impressively) some pretty sick dance moves 🪇🪩. Station operations will proceed through a series of structured observational phases, collaborative research exercises, and formal evaluation proceedings. Advisories
All activities conducted at Station 744 operate under provisional review by both the Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee (IACUC) and the Institutional Review Board (IRB).

Participants are advised of the following approved operational conditions:

* Unregulated mixing of experimental biological agents may accelerate adaptive social behaviors and disrupt homeostasis. 
* W.E.T. LAB assumes full responsibility for emergent dance phenomena.
* Cross-species pathogen transmission (e.g., COVID-19, RSV, etc.) falls outside current study objectives, and is therefore discouraged. 
* The IRB has approved limited celebratory activities associated with a participant’s annual aging event under the “Minimal Existential Risk” category.
* Should experimental conditions escalate beyond acceptable parameters, additional music will be deployed as a primary containment strategy.

Recommended Attire and Supplies
* Costuming aligned with marine life and/or scientists is strongly recommended to avoid disturbing the resident aquatic life
* Metabolic support reagents appropriate for their species classification to maintain behavioral homeostasis
* Musical instruments (optional)

Research Center Objectives
W.E.T. LAB conducts interdisciplinary studies examining:

* Adaptive behaviors across aquatic species
* Symbiosis and cooperative survival strategies
* Rapid evolutionary response to changing environments
* Social dynamics within mixed-species ecosystems
* Containment protocol resilience (currently under review)

Participants will contribute to live observational research through informal interaction, collaborative experimentation, and optional mission-based objectives distributed upon arrival.
