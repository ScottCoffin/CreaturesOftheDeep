## 🌊 Overview

Welcome to the **West Sacramento Experimental Trophic Laboratory (W.E.T. LAB)**.

This is a **team-based, marine-science-themed social game** combining:

- role-playing
- mini-game competition
- hidden mechanics
- collaborative storytelling
- a final research pitch

Participants form research labs and compete for funding through experiments, discovery, and persuasion.

---

## 🎯 Core Objective

Each team (“lab”) must:

- win mini-games (experiments)
- discover hidden funding
- deliver a compelling research proposal

💰 The lab with the **highest total funding** wins.

## Mini-Games 

---

### 🍺 Civil War Game
#### Estimated time to play:
5–8 minutes per match  

#### Research Funding Allocated per Win:
$200  

#### Rules:
- Two teams compete head-to-head
- Standard “Civil War” (beer pong variant):
  - Teams take rapid sequential shots
  - When a cup is hit:
    - Opposing team drinks
    - Cup is removed
- Continue until one team eliminates all cups
- Winning team records result

#### Scientific Framing:
Simulates:

> “Competitive feeding in a resource-limited pelagic ecosystem”

Teams represent marine populations rapidly exploiting nutrient patches under pressure, with success determined by coordination and feeding efficiency.

---

### 🦠 Plankton Survival Simulation
#### Estimated time to play:
4–6 minutes  

#### Research Funding Allocated per Win:
$200  

#### Rules:
- Each team creates one **plankton model** using molding clay
- Place models simultaneously into a tall mason jar filled with water
- Winning condition:
  - **Last to sink OR float**  
- Winner records result

#### Scientific Framing:
Simulates:

> “Morphological adaptations of plankton to optimize buoyancy and survival in the water column”

---

### 🐬 Marine Mammal Relay
#### Estimated time to play:
8–10 minutes  

#### Research Funding Allocated per Win:
$300  

#### Rules:
Teams race through a sequence of challenges:

Example sequence:
1. Put on a wetsuit (or clothing prop)
2. Perform a “breach” (jump/spin)
3. Carry an object across the room using only elbows
4. Tag next teammate

- First team to complete all relay stages wins
- All team members must participate
- Reset between rounds

#### Scientific Framing:
Simulates:

> “Energetic expenditure and coordinated movement strategies in marine mammals”

---

### 🥣 Baleen Feeding Competition
#### Estimated time to play:
5–7 minutes  

#### Research Funding Allocated per Win:
$200  

#### Rules:
- Each team receives:
  - bowl of cereal (e.g., Rice Krispies or Froot Loops)
  - milk
  - strainer or “baleen apparatus”
- Teams must simulate filter feeding:
  - separate “food” from liquid
- Constraints:
  - no hands (or limited use)
- After fixed time (or first to finish), evaluate:
  - most food captured
  - least spillage

#### Scientific Framing:
Simulates:

> “Filter-feeding efficiency in baleen whales under varying prey densities”

---

### 🧠 Fish Bowl
#### Estimated time to play:
8–10 minutes  

#### Research Funding Allocated per Win:
$300  

#### Rules:
- Standard Fish Bowl (charades/taboo hybrid)
- Teams compete head-to-head
- Use pre-made marine/science-themed prompts provided in the Fish Bowl
- For each round, teams will assign one person to be the clue-giver, while the other teammates will guess the clue
- During each round, the clue-giver will draw a clue from the Fish Bowl and try to get their team to guess it. 
- Teams should record the number of correctly-guessed clues for each round.
- Each round is 60-seconds long
- The two teams will take turns competing with each other through each round
- After each round is complete, replace the words into the Fish Bowl
- Rounds:
  1. Describe with words
  2. Act it out
  3. One-word clues
- Most correct guesses (across all rounds) wins

#### Scientific Framing:
Simulates:

> “Rapid classification and communication of species and ecological concepts under constrained signaling conditions”

---

### 🐬 Dolphin Trainer
#### Estimated time to play:
5–8 minutes  

#### Research Funding Allocated per Win:
$300  

#### Rules:
- Each team must include a **Dolphin**
- The **Dolphin** will perform tasks instructed by the other team-mates (trainers)
- Trainers may only say:
  - “Good dolphin”
  - “Bad dolphin”
- Team draws three (3) task cards at the Dolphin Trainer station
  - Each team is allowed to swap one task card with the opposing team before the start of the game 
- Objective:
  - guide the dolphin to complete each task as quickly as possible
- First team to succesfully complete all three task cards wins

#### Scientific Framing:
Simulates:

> “Behavioral conditioning and reinforcement learning in cetaceans”

---

### 🤿 Snorkel Soccer
#### Estimated time to play:
5–7 minutes  

#### Research Funding Allocated per Win:
$200  

#### Rules:
- Teams face off across a table
- Use snorkels to **blow a ping-pong ball** into opposing team's goal (cup)
- Objective:
  - score into opponent’s goal
- First to X goals (e.g., 3) wins
- No hands allowed

#### Scientific Framing:
Simulates:

> “Hydrodynamic propulsion and directional control in constrained aquatic environments”

---

### 📸 Specimen Catalog
#### Estimated time to play:
6–8 minutes  

#### Research Funding Allocated per Win:
$250  

#### Rules:
- The Specimen Catalog Station contains:
  - Polaroid camera(s)
  - 1 field notebook
- Objective:
  - catalog each team member as a “specimen” in the notebook that is present at the Station
- Requirements:
  - 1 photo per member
  - assign:
    - species name
    - key trait
    - ecological role

#### Scientific Framing:
Simulates:

> “Field documentation and taxonomic classification of novel or poorly understood organisms”
